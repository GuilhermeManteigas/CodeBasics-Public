I am an undergraduate BSc Computing Science at the University of Aberdeen. I would like to invite you to consider participating in the research project CodeBasics: An Educational Programming Language and Development Environment for Introduction to Programming. Below is some information about the project, to help you decide whether you would like to take part.

Participation in the research project is completely voluntary. You can withdraw from the project at any time, without having to give a reason.

AIMS
The aim of the project is to identify the hurdles new programmers have to overcome when learning their first programming language. Based on this identification, this research will then aim to develop a programming language targeted at people with little to no experience in programming that would address those hurdles. The final aim of the study would be to test the created language against an existing language to test whether it is significantly easier to use.

WHAT YOU WILL BE ASKED TO DO
This survey is the first part of the project and consists of answering some questions about which aspects of learning programming participants find challenging, as well as providing some demographic data.
This experiment should not take more than 20 minutes of your time.

RISKS
The questions asked in this survey are not of a distressing nature and should not trigger any adverse emotional reaction. However, in the highly unlikely event it would, participants are free to skip any questions they do not wish to answer. Additionally, participants are welcome to contact the research team to be signposted to relevant support to address any distress experienced.

The University’s Privacy Notice for Research Participants is available here
